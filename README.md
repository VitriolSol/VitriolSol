### Hey

MSc student in Wuhan. I work on things that sit between speech, vision, and language models — mostly the infrastructure around them: how to clean their training data, how to decide whether one is better than another, how to compare a dozen neural speech codecs without pretending that PESQ settings don't matter.

These are the projects I've been chipping away at:

- **[speech-codec-arena](https://github.com/VitriolSol/speech-codec-arena)** — a side-by-side benchmark harness for neural speech codecs.
- **[mm-probe](https://github.com/VitriolSol/mm-probe)** — fine-grained capability probing for multimodal LLMs; breaks a model's behavior down by counting, spatial reasoning, OCR, attribute binding.
- **[vl-data-engine](https://github.com/VitriolSol/vl-data-engine)** — pipeline for cleaning vision-language instruction-tuning data.

Things I think about most of the time: why VLMs fall off a cliff past count = 4, whether a codec can be "good for speech LMs" without being good at reconstruction, and how much of data cleaning is the same three functions in different outfits.

Tinkering, reading, writing. Sometimes all three at once.
